GTK+ Developers

About the GTK+ team

    GTK+ has a large development community, as can be seen by the lists of contributors in the GTK+-2.0.0 and GTK+-2.2.0 release announcements. However, there are some people that can be identified as owners of one piece of code or the other. These people are listed below.

Team members

    Jonathan Blandford <jrb redhat com
    Maintains: GtkTreeView (along with Kristian). Wrote GtkTreeView originally.
    Matthias Clasen <maclas gmx de>
    Maintains: gdk-pixbuf, GtkUIManager, documentation. Works on almost all parts of Glib and GTK+
    Tim Janik <tmj gtk org>k;
    Maintains: GObject. Long-time core GTK+ maintainer.
    Tor Lillqvist <tml iki fi>
    Maintains: Win32 port of GTK+.
    Federico Mena Quintero <federico ximian com>
    Maintains: new GtkFileChooser. Past maintainer of gdk-pixbuf. Long-time bug-fixer.
    Kristian Rietveld <kris gtk org>
    Maintains: GtkTreeView (along with Jonathan), GtkComboBox. Wrote GtkComboBox, completion support for GtkEntry, GtkFilterModel
    Soeren Sandmann <sandmann daimi au dk>
    Maintains: GtkToolbar, default appearance of GTK+. Has done a lot of work on user interface details of things like GtkMenu.
    Owen Taylor <otaylor redhat com>
    Maintains: Pango, X11 port of GDK, core widget system. General dogsbody.
    Sebastian Wilhelmi <seppi seppi de>
    Maintains: Glib thread support

Other credits

    Peter Matthis started it all, with help from Spencer Kimball and Josh Macdonald. Marius Vollmer taught us how to make GTK+ work for language bindings. Lars Hamann and Stefan Jeske added all sorts of features to the core widgets. Carsten Haitzler designed and did the first implementation of theme support. Shawn Amundsons did release management for GTK+-1.0 and GTK+-1.2. More recently, Havoc Pennington created the GtkTextView widget based on the Tk text widget. Elliot Lee and Alex Larsson created the linux-fb backend. Erwann Chenede added multihead support to GDK and GTK+. Hans Breuer continues to contribute enormous amounts of useful work on the Win32 port of GTK+. Abigail Brady, Hans Breuer, Sivaraj Doddannan, Dov Grobgeld, Karl Koehler, Theppitak Karoonboonyanan, Noah Levitt, Eric Mader, Roozbeh Pournader, and Changwoo Ryu are just a few of the contributors to Pango.
